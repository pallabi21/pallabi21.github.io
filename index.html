Projects

Geospatial Analysis of Hurricane Florence Across the U.S. (2018)

Go to Project

This map illustrates the trajectory and intensity of Hurricane Florence, a powerful Cape Verde hurricane that struck the Carolinas in September 2018, causing catastrophic flooding and destruction. The storm's path is visualized using directional markers ("<"), color-coded based on wind speed variations, helping to analyze when and where the hurricane intensified. The U.S. map is displayed in blue, while a color scale (legend) on the right represents the range of wind speeds, providing insight into the storm's strength at different locations. Latitude and longitude grids enhance spatial context, allowing for a clearer understanding of the hurricane's movement. By analyzing this map, we can track where Hurricane Florence originated, how it evolved over time, and the regions most affected by its impact. This geospatial visualization serves as a crucial tool for disaster analysis, meteorological research, and emergency preparedness in understanding the devastating effects of hurricanes.
